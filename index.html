Crop Cultivation Guides

Detailed guidance for successful crop production in Sri Lanka

Rice Cultivation

Water: 1200-1800 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 80-150 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Grown extensively across all climate zones (Wet, Intermediate, Dry). Two main cultivation seasons: Maha (Sept/Oct - Mar/Apr) and Yala (Apr/May - Aug/Sept).

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear weeds, level the field, bund repair. Plow/rotavate to loosen soil (wet or dry tillage).

Select quality seeds, soak for 24 hrs, incubate for 24 hrs.

Broadcast pre-germinated seeds or transplant 2-3 seedlings per hill (20-25 cm spacing).

Maintain appropriate water levels (2-5 cm early, 5-10 cm flowering), drain before harvesting.

Apply basal fertilizers (TSP, MOP) before planting. Apply Urea in splits based on plant growth stages.

Monitor for stem borer, leaf folder, blast, brown spot. Use IPM strategies.

Harvest when 80-85% of grains are golden. Dry paddy to 14% moisture.

Maize Cultivation

Water: 500-800 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 80-120 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Best suited for Dry and Intermediate Zones. Grown in both Maha (Sept/Oct - Mar/Apr) and Yala (Apr/May - Aug/Sept) seasons. Requires warm conditions, sensitive to waterlogging and frost.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear weeds, plow to 20-25 cm depth for fine tilth.

Plant seeds 5 cm deep, 25-30 cm apart within rows, 75 cm between rows.

Irrigate every 7-10 days if rainfall is insufficient, especially during tasseling/silking and grain filling.

Apply NPK at planting, top dress with Urea 3-4 weeks after planting. Follow local recommendations.

Use pre-emergent herbicides or manual weeding.

Monitor for Fall Armyworm, stem borer, leaf blight. Implement timely control measures.

Harvest when cobs are dry and kernels are hard (90-120 days).

Green Gram Cultivation

Water: 300-500 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 20-40 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Ideal for Dry and Intermediate Zones. Grown in Maha and Yala seasons, often as a short-term or rotation crop. Prefers warm, dry conditions; sensitive to excessive moisture during flowering and pod maturity.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear weeds, plow lightly for a fine seedbed.

Plant seeds 3-5 cm deep, 10-15 cm apart within rows, 30-45 cm between rows.

Usually rain-fed; irrigate lightly during dry spells, especially at flowering and pod filling. Avoid over-watering.

Apply TSP at planting; minimal nitrogen needed due to nitrogen fixation.

Use herbicides or manual weeding, especially early on.

Monitor for aphids, pod borer, yellow mosaic virus. Apply appropriate measures.

Harvest dry, mature pods in multiple pickings (60-75 days).

Chili Cultivation

Water: 500-700 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 80-120 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Widely grown in Dry and Intermediate Zones. Cultivated in both Maha and Yala seasons; prefers dry conditions during flowering and fruiting to reduce disease. Requires warm temperatures; sensitive to frost and excessive humidity.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear weeds, plow thoroughly, create raised beds (15-20 cm high) for drainage.

Sow seeds in nursery beds or trays.

Transplant 30-45-day-old seedlings (45-60 cm between rows, 30-45 cm between plants).

Irrigate regularly, avoiding overhead irrigation during flowering/fruiting.

Apply basal NPK; top dress with Nitrogen and Potassium during vegetative growth and fruiting.

Use mulching, manual weeding, or herbicides.

Monitor for thrips, mites, aphids, whiteflies, leaf curl virus, anthracnose, bacterial wilt. Use IPM and nursery nets.

Pick mature blue or red chilies in multiple rounds over months.

Onion Cultivation

Water: 400-600 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 80-100 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Grown mainly in Dry and Intermediate Zones during Yala season (Apr/May - Aug/Sept). Prefers warm days and cooler nights; sensitive to excessive moisture during bulb formation.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear weeds, plow and harrow for fine tilth, form raised beds (15 cm high).

Plant small bulbs (sets) or transplant 6-8-week-old seedlings (10-15 cm apart, 15-20 cm between rows).

Irrigate regularly to keep soil moist; reduce significantly as bulbs mature to prevent rot.

Apply basal NPK; top dress Nitrogen during vegetative growth and Potassium before bulb formation.

Manual weeding, critical in early stages.

Monitor for onion maggots, thrips, purple blotch, basal rot. Use integrated control.

Harvest when leaves yellow and tops fall (100-120 days); cure for 7-10 days in dry, shaded, ventilated area.

Tomato Cultivation

Water: 400-600 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 80-120 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Grown in all zones, primarily in Intermediate and Dry Zones during Maha season. Prefers warm days and cooler nights; sensitive to high humidity and frost. Wet Zone requires raised beds and disease management.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Plow deeply, prepare fine tilth, form raised beds for drainage.

Sow seeds in nursery beds or trays; protect from heavy rain and direct sun.

Transplant 4-6-week-old seedlings (60-90 cm between rows, 45-60 cm between plants).

Maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during flowering and fruiting; use drip irrigation.

Apply basal NPK; top dress Nitrogen and Potassium during vegetative growth and flowering/fruiting. Calcium is key.

Support plants with stakes or trellises to keep fruit off the ground.

Remove side shoots (suckers) for main stem growth and airflow.

Use mulching, manual weeding, or herbicides.

Monitor for fruit borer, whiteflies, aphids, leaf miners, early/late blight, bacterial wilt, leaf curl virus. Use integrated management.

Harvest fruits at desired color/maturity in multiple pickings.

Banana Cultivation

Water: 1000-1500 mm annually
Fertilizer (N): 150-300 kg/ha annually

Climate Requirements

Grown in all zones, with better yields in Wet and Intermediate Zones. Requires warm, humid conditions; sensitive to frost and prolonged dry spells. Year-round cultivation possible with irrigation.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear land, dig pits (60 x 60 x 60 cm), add organic matter.

Use healthy suckers or tissue-cultured plants.

Plant suckers in pits (2.5-3 m apart depending on variety).

Provide regular irrigation, especially during dry periods; avoid waterlogging.

Apply NPK and organic manure regularly; Potassium is critical during bunch development.

Remove excess suckers, allowing 1-2 follower suckers for the next crop.

Use mulching or manual weeding to suppress weeds.

Monitor for banana weevil, nematodes, Panama wilt, Sigatoka leaf spot, Banana Bunchy Top Virus. Use clean planting material and treatments.

Harvest bunches when fruits are well-filled but blue (8-15 months from planting).

Mango Cultivation

Water: 800-1200 mm annually
Fertilizer (N): 200-500 g/tree/year

Climate Requirements

Grown in all zones, with better flowering and fruiting in Dry and Intermediate Zones. Requires a distinct dry period (2-3 months) before flowering for good fruit set; high rainfall or humidity during flowering reduces yield. Prefers warm, frost-free conditions.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Clear land, dig pits (1 x 1 x 1 m) well before planting.

Use grafted plants from recommended varieties.

Plant grafted plants in pits, ensuring graft union is above soil (8 x 8 m to 12 x 12 m spacing).

Water young plants regularly; stop irrigation 2-3 months before flowering to induce it, resume after fruit set.

Apply NPK and organic manure based on tree age and yield; Phosphorus and Potassium aid flowering/fruiting.

Prune to maintain shape, remove dead/diseased branches, improve airflow and light.

Keep tree base weed-free; mulching is beneficial.

Monitor for mango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, anthracnose, powdery mildew. Integrated management is key during flowering/fruiting.

Harvest mature fruits at correct stage (color change, shoulder filling).

Cabbage Cultivation

Water: 300-500 mm per season
Fertilizer (N): 80-150 kg/ha

Climate Requirements

Best suited for cooler climates in Upcountry and mid-country regions. Requires cool to moderate temperatures for head formation; high temperatures cause loose, poor-quality heads. Sensitive to heat and water stress; grown year-round in suitable microclimates.

Growth Timeline

Cultivation Steps

Plow thoroughly, prepare fine, firm seedbed; form raised beds in waterlogged areas.

Sow seeds in nursery beds or trays.

Transplant 3-4-week-old seedlings (45-60 cm between rows, 30-45 cm between plants).

Maintain consistent soil moisture, especially during head formation.

Apply basal NPK and organic manure; top dress Nitrogen and Potassium during vegetative growth and head formation. Address Boron deficiency.

Manual weeding, crucial in early stages.

Monitor for cabbage worm, diamondback moth, aphids, black rot, downy mildew, clubroot. Use pesticides, fungicides, and crop rotation.

Harvest firm heads at desired size before splitting (60-120 days depending on variety/climate).

Comprehensive information for profitable animal husbandry

Cattle Management

Optimal Temp: 16-24°C
Water: 40-60L per day

Climate Considerations

Found across all climate zones. Productivity affected by high temperatures and humidity. Heat stress can reduce milk yield and reproductive efficiency.

Milk Production Factors

Management Aspects

Provide adequate shelter to protect from sun, rain, and wind. Ensure good ventilation. Maintain clean and dry housing to prevent foot problems and mastitis.

Provide a balanced diet of blue fodder (pasture, cultivated grasses, browse), hay/silage (especially in dry periods), and concentrate feeds based on age, physiological state (growth, lactation, pregnancy), and production level. Ensure access to clean water at all times.

Implement a regular vaccination program against prevalent diseases (e.g., Foot and Mouth Disease). Conduct regular deworming and external parasite control. Monitor for signs of illness and seek veterinary attention promptly. Practice good sanitation and biosecurity.

Use healthy breeding animals. Implement appropriate breeding programs (natural service or artificial insemination) considering genetic improvement and climate adaptation. Monitor reproductive cycles and manage breeding for optimal calving intervals.

Regular grooming, hoof trimming, and observation of animal behavior are important for detecting health issues early.

Poultry Management (Chickens, Ducks)

Optimal Temp: 18-24°C
Water: Fresh water constantly

Climate Considerations

High temperatures and humidity cause heat stress, leading to reduced feed intake, growth rate, egg production, and increased mortality. Rainy seasons increase humidity and disease risk.

Egg Production Factors

Management Aspects

Provide well-ventilated housing with adequate space per bird. Orient sheds east-west to minimize direct sunlight. Use suitable litter material and keep it dry. Control temperature and humidity where possible.

Provide balanced feed formulated for the specific type and age of poultry. Ensure access to clean, cool water at all times. Adjust feeding times and amounts based on temperature.

Implement strict vaccination program for common diseases (Ranikhet/Newcastle Disease, Gumboro). Practice rigorous biosecurity measures. Regular deworming for free-range birds.

Select healthy breeding stock. For layers, provide adequate nesting space and lighting program. Manage stocking density.

Regular cleaning and disinfection of sheds and equipment. Proper waste disposal. Daily monitoring of bird behavior.

Buffaloes Management

Heat Tolerance: More tolerant than cattle
Requirement: Wallowing areas essential

Climate Considerations

Common in Dry and Intermediate Zones. Benefit from wallowing areas (mud/water) to regulate body temperature. Need dry resting areas during rains. Green fodder availability fluctuates with seasons.

Milk Composition

Management Aspects

Simple shelters providing shade and rain protection. Access to wallowing tank or muddy area is highly beneficial. Ensure clean resting areas.

Primarily graze on natural pastures, paddy stubble, and crop residues. Supplementary feeding important for working animals, lactating cows, and growing stock during dry spells.

Implement vaccination program. Regular deworming, especially in communal grazing areas. Monitor for external parasites and skin conditions.

Utilize healthy bulls for natural service or consider artificial insemination. Monitor heat cycles and breeding.

Allow access to wallowing areas. Provide mineral supplements. Regular observation for health issues.

Sheep Management

Tolerance: Tolerant to semi-arid conditions
Avoid: Excessive wetness

Climate Considerations

More common in drier areas (Dry and Intermediate Zones). Excessive wetness and humidity can lead to foot rot and internal parasite issues. Seasonal availability of pasture is a major factor.

Wool/Fiber Quality

Management Aspects

Provide simple, dry shelters that protect from rain and predators, especially at night. Ensure good ventilation.

Primarily graze on natural vegetation, crop residues. Supplementary feeding with concentrates needed during dry seasons or poor grazing.

Implement regular deworming program, rotating dewormers. Control external parasites (ticks, mites). Monitor for foot problems, especially in wet conditions.

Manage breeding to optimize lambing rates. Select healthy breeding animals. Monitor lambing and provide care for ewes and lambs.

Regular observation of the flock. Hoof trimming as needed. Shearing for certain breeds.

Pigs Management

Sensitivity: Highly susceptible to heat stress
Requirement: Wallowing/cooling systems needed

Climate Considerations

Require careful temperature management. Cannot sweat effectively - need cool housing, wallowing areas, or cooling systems (misters, fans) in warm weather. Housing must protect from direct sun and rain.

Growth Rate Factors

Management Aspects

Comfortable, well-ventilated housing. Concrete or easily cleanable floors. Include wallowing areas or cooling systems. Separate pens for different age groups.

Balanced diet (swill or commercial feed) formulated for different growth stages. Ensure access to clean water, preferably using nipple drinkers. Adjust feed quantity based on temperature.

Vaccination program for relevant diseases (e.g., Swine Fever). Maintain strict biosecurity. Implement parasite control. Good sanitation is vital.

Select healthy breeding stock. Manage breeding cycles and farrowing carefully. Provide farrowing pens for sows.

Regular cleaning and disinfection of pens. Provide enrichment materials. Handle animals calmly.

Goats Management

Tolerance: Tolerant to hot, dry conditions
Sensitivity: Sensitive to prolonged wetness

Climate Considerations

Widely adapted across all zones, particularly drier regions. Prefer Browse to grazing. Sensitive to prolonged wet and cold conditions (can lead to pneumonia and foot problems).

Milk/Meat Production

Management Aspects

Dry, draft-free shelters that protect from rain and cold. Elevated slatted floors in wet areas. Ensure good ventilation.

Primarily browse on shrubs, trees, and weeds. Can graze pastures and consume crop residues. Provide supplementary feed during fodder scarcity.

Regular deworming program. Control external parasites. Monitor for respiratory diseases and foot rot. Vaccinate against prevalent diseases.

Select healthy breeding animals. Manage breeding seasons and monitor kidding. Provide care for does and kids.

Regular observation of the flock. Hoof trimming is essential. Provide Browse opportunities if possible.

Other Livestock (Rabbits, Ducks, Turkeys)

Requirement: Varies by species
Action: Research specific needs

General Considerations

Requirements vary significantly by species. Protection from extreme temperatures, rain, and wind is necessary. Adequate ventilation important in all climates. Seasonal changes impact feed availability and disease prevalence.

Management Aspects

Species-appropriate housing that is safe, clean, and protects from weather and predators. Ensure adequate space and ventilation.

Balanced diet specific to the animal's nutritional needs, age, and purpose (meat, eggs). Ensure continuous access to clean water.

Research common diseases for the specific animal type. Implement preventative measures (vaccination if available, sanitation). Seek veterinary advice from specialists.

Understand the breeding cycles and requirements for the specific animal type.

Regular cleaning of housing. Proper handling and observation of animals.

Frequently Asked Questions

The optimal time for paddy cultivation depends on the region and season. In the Maha season (October-February), planting is typically done in October-November, while in the Yala season (April-August), planting is done in April-May.
To prevent heat stress in dairy cattle: provide shade and good ventilation, ensure access to clean cool water at all times, adjust feeding times to cooler parts of the day, consider sprinklers or fans, and select heat-tolerant breeds when possible.
Essential poultry vaccinations include: Newcastle disease (day 1, 14, and booster), Infectious bronchitis (day 1 and booster), Gumboro disease (day 7-14 and booster), and Fowl pox (6-8 weeks). Consult a veterinarian for a complete vaccination schedule.
